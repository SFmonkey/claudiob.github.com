I started programming basic at five, on a sharp mz-700 connected to the TV set. At eight, my father bought our first pc, a 286 intel processor. In 1992, with the first modem, I was playing around with gopher and bbs set up in my friends' houses. The www exploded in 1994, and I started building web sites in html 2 and flash 4. After fifteen years, I am still trying to find the right form to involve all of this, with style.

I have left many traces on the web. First of all, there is my inconceivable love for music: music I play, music I used to play, music I listen to, music I create. Then my passion underground railways and typography.

There are plenty of thoughts I collected, and nice places I visited. There are unforgettable moments I lived.

There is my scientific research, my my Ph.D. thesis, my pieces of code and also what Google says about me.

And there is so much more left off-line that I will share with you, fellow stranger, the day we will meet.
